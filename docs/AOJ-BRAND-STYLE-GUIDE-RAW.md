AOJ BRAND GUIDELINES
BRAND OVERVIEW

1.1 What is AOJ? 

At Anatomy of Joy, we create personalized, illustrated keepsakes that celebrate the stories and emotions worth holding on to. 

Our designs are rooted in storytelling—whether it’s a personal journey, a company milestone, a special place, or a moment in time. Each piece is created intentionally and with heart, transforming intangible moments into tangible keepsakes.

1.2. Brand universe

Story, emotion, personal, joy, human, keepsake, art, moments, warm, handmade, thoughtful, premium, unique

1.3. Brand pillars

Story: Rooted in emotion, each piece reflects personal moments, relationships and experiences. We design to capture what is felt, not just what is seen.

Keepsake: Crafted with care and quality, we create objects that hold meaning—and are made to be held onto. 

Blank Canvas: A quiet, open space for your story—shaped by what matters most to you.

5.2 Image direction

Context:
Grounded lifestyle moments (real, human, everyday)
Product-forward compositions (expressive, dynamic, creative)

Mood:
Light, airy and bright (avoid dull or heavy imagery)
Soft, diffused natural light
Clean compositions with minimal props and distractions
Styling that feels lived-in (not overly editorial)

Colors + textures:
Light, neutral, warm-toned palette
Avoid dark, moody tones (e.g. off-white > beige > brown)
Organic textures and natural fabrics (cotton, linen, paper, wood)
Avoid formal styling, officewear, or synthetic materials
The product is the primary source of contrast and color
The environment should support, not compete

VISUAL LANGUAGE: How it all comes together

6.1 Concept: “Anatomy Of A Story”

At Anatomy Of joy, the idea of “anatomy” defines our visual language. Stories are made of words and emotions, woven together to create something unique and personal. 

Words are treated as an intentional design element—wrapping and flowing through the layout mirroring both the unfolding of a story, and the flow of a silk scarf.

Arrows, marks and illustrated elements are used as tools to point out parts of an image or page to highlight or draw further attention.
