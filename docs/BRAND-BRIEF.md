# Brand Brief — Anatomy of Joy

Source: Shivantika's email (Apr 17, 2026) + AOJ Brand Style Guide + Etsy shop research

## What is Anatomy of Joy?

Personalized, illustrated keepsakes that celebrate the stories and emotions worth holding on to. Each piece is custom — rooted in storytelling, whether it's a personal journey, a company milestone, a special place, or a moment in time. Transforming intangible moments into tangible keepsakes.

**Domain:** anatomyofjoy.co
**Etsy:** etsy.com/shop/AnatomyOfJoy
**Instagram:** @theanatomyofjoy
**Founder email:** shivantika@anatomyofjoy.co

## Working Tagline

"Where your stories find a forever."

## Brand Pillars

1. **Story** — Rooted in emotion, each piece reflects personal moments, relationships and experiences. Designed to capture what is felt, not just what is seen.
2. **Keepsake** — Crafted with care and quality. Objects that hold meaning, made to be held onto.
3. **Blank Canvas** — A quiet, open space for your story, shaped by what matters most to you.

## Brand Universe (Keywords)

Story, emotion, personal, joy, human, keepsake, art, moments, warm, handmade, thoughtful, premium, unique

## Typography

### AOJ Signature Script
- Usage: premade logo-like icons ONLY (not used as a regular "font")
- Only for: logo, product/collection names
- Never for: brand communications, body text, headers

### Primary: Pasta and Wine
- Usage: headers, formal contexts
- Uppercase and lowercase
- Context: website headers, decks, formal text
- Role: equivalent to Whiled's "Meek" — the display/personality font

### Body: DM Sans
- Usage: paragraphs, informational text (more than 1-2 lines)
- Uppercase and lowercase, various weights
- Context: website body, descriptions, any informational text
- Role: equivalent to Whiled's "Beatrice" — clean, readable system font

### Display: Pasta and Wine + Infamous (+ Quisas?)
- Usage: expressive, creative, varied
- Context: social media, campaign images
- "Infamous" is a messy handwriting font — for personality moments

## Imagery Direction

### Visual Tone
Expressive, human, artistic

### Positioning Spectrum
- Real ← **X** → Staged (leaning real)
- Grounded ← **X** → Dreamy (leaning grounded)
- Minimal → **X** ← Expressive (leaning expressive)
- Intentional ← **X** → Decorative (strongly intentional)
- Quiet ← **X** → Quirky (leaning quiet)

### Image Direction
**Context:**
- Grounded lifestyle moments (real, human, everyday)
- Product-forward compositions (expressive, dynamic, creative)

**Mood:**
- Light, airy and bright (avoid dull or heavy imagery)
- Soft, diffused natural light
- Clean compositions with minimal props and distractions
- Styling that feels lived-in (not overly editorial)

**Colors + Textures:**
- Light, neutral, warm-toned palette
- Avoid dark, moody tones (off-white > beige > brown)
- Organic textures and natural fabrics (cotton, linen, paper, wood)
- Avoid formal styling, officewear, or synthetic materials
- The product is the primary source of contrast and color
- The environment should support, not compete

## Visual Language: "Anatomy Of A Story"

The concept of "anatomy" defines the visual language. Stories are made of words and emotions, woven together.

### Text as Design Element
Words are treated as an intentional design element — wrapping and flowing through the layout, mirroring both the unfolding of a story and the flow of a silk scarf.

### Visual Library (Hand-Drawn Elements)
- **Arrows + marks** — used to point out, highlight, draw attention (available in black and white, Google Drive folder)
- **Dividers** — hand-drawn section dividers (8 variants available, Google Drive folder)
- **Frames + borders** — for testimonials and callouts

These hand-drawn elements are a key differentiator from Whiled's colored-border approach. AOJ uses illustrated, organic marks instead of geometric color lines.

## Build Notes

### Custom Order Flow
Unlike Whiled's standard e-commerce (add to cart → checkout), AOJ needs a **custom order flow** since each scarf is personalized. This likely means:
- Product page explains the process
- Customer provides their story/location details
- Shivantika creates the custom illustration
- Customer approves → production → shipping

This is a critical UX consideration — the "buy" button isn't just "add to cart." It's the start of a creative collaboration.
